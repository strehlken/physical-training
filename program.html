Programming Notes & Rationale
Bench Press Rehab Protocol
Bench is running on its own track independent of the main program. The rule is simple: +5 lbs per session, twice per week, 3×8. You did 95×8 yesterday. The projected path across this block runs from 100 to roughly 145, but the pain rule governs everything — 0-3/10 during the set, baseline recovery by next morning. If a weight doesn't pass, repeat it. If it fails twice, drop 10 lbs and rebuild. The spreadsheet numbers are projections, not mandates.
Pull-Up Rehab Progression
You started ROM-restricted pull-ups yesterday, so you're about 1 week into the 8-week timeline. Weeks 1-2: stay at partial ROM, stopping short of dead hang. Weeks 3-4: begin extending range — lower further into the hang each session, guided by pain response. Weeks 5-6: approaching near-full or full ROM for triples and quads. By the end of this block, the next program can treat pull-ups as a normal movement. Bench and pull-ups are on separate days throughout to avoid compounding load on the biceps tendon.
Squat Strategy — Why Twice a Week
The 6-month target is 300 for reps from a current estimated max around 285-290. Squatting once a week leaves reps on the table. Heavy Sunday/Monday drives strength (top set + backoffs, RPE ~8). Saturday volume builds work capacity at lighter loads. When you pick Sunday over Monday, you're squatting back-to-back days — that works as long as Saturday stays honest at programmed weights. Monday is the better option when schedule allows.
Deadlift Path to 400
Estimated current max is ~335-340. This block builds to a smooth 325×2 by week 5, which puts estimated max around 345-355. At that rate, 400 is reachable in 3-4 more blocks (roughly 5-6 months). No RDLs in the deadlift session — those are on squat day to spread posterior chain load. Front squats stay after deads for quad drive and positioning without hammering the back.
OHP Recalibrated
The previous block peaked at 102.5×4-5, but you hit 135 for two singles this week — that's a big jump. OHP track has been recalibrated upward: starting at 110×5 and building to 130×2-3 by the push week. This keeps you working in a productive range instead of coasting through easy sets.
RDLs — Reintroduced With a Leash
The last block dropped RDLs after your back flagged something. They're back at 175 to start (not 185) — one conservative week to confirm tolerance before loading up. If your back speaks up at any point, pull them immediately. The cost of starting 10 lbs light is nothing; the cost of re-tweaking is weeks. Good mornings on Saturday serve as a second hamstring stimulus at very low load — drop those too if back is unhappy.
Equipment & General Rules
Equipment: single barbell (45 lb), squat rack, flat bench, pull-up bar. No dumbbells, no machines, no cables. All accessories are barbell or bodyweight only.

RPE: top sets should feel like ~8 (could do about 2 more reps). If something feels like a grinder, it's too heavy — drop 5 lbs and take the clean reps.

Session time: everything is designed for ≤45 minutes. If you're running long, cut optional core first, then trim a backoff set.

Rows: on trial basis only. If barbell rows aggravate the shoulder at any point, drop them entirely for this block. OHP + bench rehab + presswork is sufficient upper body volume while the shoulder heals.
